\begin{abstract}
We propose a four-field bone-remodeling formulation that co-evolves displacement $\mathbf{u}$, apparent density $\rho$, log-fabric $\mathbf{L} = \ln \mathbf{A}$, and a nonlocal mechanotransductive stimulus $S$. The elastic response follows a fabric-guided orthotropic law, where stiffness tensors are constructed from the spectral decomposition of $\mathbf{L}$. A cycle-weighted strain-energy driver is filtered by a diffusion--reaction stimulus with a smooth saturating (lazy-zone) mechanostat; density evolves via bounded formation/resorption kinetics with diffusive regularization; and log-fabric relaxes toward a trace-free logarithmic target constructed from cycle-weighted \emph{deviatoric} stress outer-products, with spatial diffusion and activity gating. Time integration is fully implicit Backward--Euler; space is discretized by conforming $H^1$ finite elements in a unified weak-form notation. Nonlinear coupling is solved by a partitioned block Gauss--Seidel sweep (mechanics $\rightarrow$ fabric $\rightarrow$ stimulus $\rightarrow$ density) wrapped in Pulay (type-II) mixing (DIIS form) with scaled Tikhonov regularization, step limiting, and adaptive history restarts. A single smoothing policy used across clamping and normalizations ensures differentiability and preserves coercivity, while the solver architecture yields stable, mesh-robust convergence without monolithic Jacobians.
\end{abstract}
