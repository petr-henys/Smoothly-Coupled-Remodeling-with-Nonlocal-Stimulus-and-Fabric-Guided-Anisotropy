%% Abstract
\begin{abstract}
We propose a three-field, smoothly regularized continuum framework for bone remodeling that couples (i) density adaptation, (ii) evolution of anisotropy in logarithmic fabric space, and (iii) a nonlocal mechanotransductive stimulus represented as an explicit diffusion--reaction field. The central novelty is a numerically robust treatment of fabric-driven orthotropy that remains well-defined across eigenvalue degeneracies: all spectral quantities required by the constitutive mapping and by the trace-free fabric target are evaluated via Sylvester spectral projectors (without eigenvectors) and smoothly blended to the isotropic and transversely isotropic limits while enforcing partition of unity, thereby preventing basis flips and discontinuous updates. Mechanical loading influences remodeling through a quasi-static elastic response that drives the stimulus, whereas density evolves through bounded formation/resorption kinetics augmented by spatial regularization to suppress checkerboarding and mesh sensitivity. The fully implicit coupled update is advanced by a modular partitioned block Gauss--Seidel strategy stabilized by Pulay/DIIS (type-II Anderson) mixing with step limiting and adaptive history management, avoiding monolithic Jacobians while maintaining stable convergence. We provide a reproducible reference implementation with scalable parallel I/O and automated verification, and we demonstrate the method on benchmark tests and a population-CT-derived proximal femur case study as a robustness demonstration on anatomical geometry, including sensitivity analyses that quantify the effect of plausible loading variations on simulated density redistribution.
\end{abstract}
