\section*{Anisotropy analysis}
\label{sec:anisotropy-analysis}

\paragraph{Motivation.}
The fabric state enters the constitutive law through bounded eigenvalue ratios and principal directions (\S\ref{subsec:direction-nd}): the eigenvectors of $\mathbf{L}$ define the orthotropic material frame, while its eigenvalues determine the stiffness contrast.
To verify that the anisotropy submodel behaves as intended and to quantify parameter sensitivity, we perform a compact sweep over the key fabric parameters $(\tau_A,\gamma_F)$ in \eqref{eq:L-strong-nd}--\eqref{eq:M-hat-nd}.
Here ``reorientation'' refers to changes of the structural tensor field driven by remodeling (formation/resorption), not to a rigid rotation of material points.

\paragraph{Sweep setup (box benchmark).}
We reuse the box benchmark setup of \S\ref{sec:regularization-analysis}, but run with fixed time step $\Delta t=1~\mathrm{d}$ and a short horizon $T=30~\mathrm{d}$.
Adaptive time stepping is disabled and all parameters other than $\tau_A$ and $\gamma_F$ are held fixed.
To probe alignment dynamics, the initial $\mathbf{L}(\cdot,0)$ is chosen as a reproducible random symmetric, trace-free field, ensuring it is not trivially aligned with the mechanically induced target.
We sweep $\tau_A\in\{30,120,480\}$~days (baseline: $120$~days) and $\gamma_F\in\{0.5,1,2\}$ (baseline: $1$), yielding $3\times 3=9$ runs.

\paragraph{Anisotropy metrics.}
Let $\mathrm{sym}(\mathbf{L})=\sum_{i=1}^3 \ell_i\,\mathbf{P}_i$ and define fabric eigenvalues $m_i:=\exp(\ell_i)$ (for the trace-free log-fabric target, $\prod_i m_i=1$).
We summarize anisotropy magnitude by the scale-invariant anisotropy index computed pointwise from $\{m_i\}$ and then averaged over the domain:
\[
A \;:=\; \sqrt{\frac{3}{2}}\,\frac{\|\mathrm{dev}(\mathbf{m})\|_2}{\|\mathbf{m}\|_2}\in[0,1],
\qquad
\mathbf{m}:=(m_1,m_2,m_3).
\]
Here $\mathrm{dev}(\mathbf{m}):=\mathbf{m}-\bar m\,\mathbf{1}$ with $\bar m:=\tfrac13(m_1+m_2+m_3)$.
Unlike the raw principal directions $(\mathbf{n}_1,\mathbf{n}_2,\mathbf{n}_3)$, these scalars provide a well-defined ``strength'' of anisotropy even when eigenvectors become ill-conditioned near isotropy.
For completeness we also monitor eigenvalue ratios (e.g.\ $m_{\max}/m_{\min}$), but under the trace-free normalization they provide the same parameter ranking as $A$ and are therefore omitted from the main figure.

\paragraph{Observed trends.}
Figure~\ref{fig:fabric_diagnostic} shows the expected and physically interpretable sensitivities.
Decreasing $\tau_A$ increases $A$ because the relaxation term in \eqref{eq:L-strong-nd} drives $\mathbf{L}$ closer to the mechanically informed target within the fixed horizon $T$; conversely, for $\tau_A\gg T$ the fabric remains close to its initial state and anisotropy growth is weak.
Increasing $\gamma_F$ strengthens anisotropy through \eqref{eq:M-hat-nd} by amplifying the eigenvalue contrast of the target and thereby of the relaxed state.
In this benchmark the mechanically induced $\bar{\mathbf{Q}}$ is strongly directional under compression, so changes in the fabric response are dominated by anisotropy magnitude rather than by spatially varying reorientation.
