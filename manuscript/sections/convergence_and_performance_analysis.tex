\section{Convergence and Performance Analysis}
\label{app:convergence}
\subsection*{Coupling convergence and recorded performance}

All simulations in this section use the partitioned fixed-point solver described in \S\ref{sec:fp-aa}.
The coupling state is
\[
\mathbf{x}=(\mathbf{L},S,\rho),
\]
i.e.\ log-fabric $\mathbf{L}$, stimulus $S$, and density $\rho$, packed and mixed in this exact order (fabric $\rightarrow$ stimulus $\rightarrow$ density).
Within each coupling subiteration we apply a block Gauss--Seidel sweep in the order mechanics $\rightarrow$ fabric $\rightarrow$ stimulus $\rightarrow$ density.
Mechanics field(s) (e.g.\ displacements) and derived drivers are treated as internal quantities of the mechanics block and are not part of the fixed-point iterate.
Coupling convergence is measured by the scaled relative Picard residual $r_{\mathrm{Pic}}$ in \eqref{eq:fp-picard-res}, i.e.\ on the coefficient vectors of $(\mathbf{L},S,\rho)$ only.

Performance metrics are recorded per time-step attempt (accepted or rejected) and per coupling subiteration.
For the convergence study we aggregate, over accepted steps, the linear-solver times and iteration counts for the four blocks (mechanics, fabric, stimulus, density), together with peak memory.

\subsection*{Spatio-temporal discretization convergence}

We quantify spatial/temporal consistency using field–norm pair errors on a mesh-refinement sequence (fixed $\Delta t$) and on a fixed mesh (varying $\Delta t$).
Errors are computed as absolute $L^2$ and $H^1$--seminorms (componentwise for tensors) and evaluated on the finer mesh of each refinement pair using non-matching transfer.

\paragraph{Meshes and notation.}
Let $\{\mathcal{T}_i\}_{i=1}^L$ be a family of meshes on $\Omega$ obtained by uniform refinement, with characteristic sizes $h_i$.
On each $\mathcal{T}_i$ we solve the coupled problem to a fixed time $T$ with \emph{the same} $\Delta t$ and model parameters.
Denote by
\[
\mathbf{L}_i,\quad S_i,\quad \rho_i
\]
the discrete fields on level $i$ in their conforming $H^1$ spaces $T_{h_i},Q_{h_i},Q_{h_i}$.
For the box benchmark we report a dimensional estimate $h_i \approx L_x/N_i$, where $L_x$ is the box extent and $N_i$ the nominal resolution parameter.

\paragraph{Field–norm pair errors on the finer mesh.}
For two consecutive levels $(H,h)=(i,i{+}1)$ we evaluate errors on the finer mesh in a \emph{higher–order observation space}.
For any field $X\in\{\mathbf{L},S,\rho\}$ we construct $W_{h}^{(X)}$ by raising the polynomial degree by $\Delta p=2$, transfer $X_H$ to $W_{h}^{(X)}$ by non-matching interpolation, and interpolate $X_h$ into the same space.
With $\mathcal{I}_{H\to h}$ denoting the non-matching interpolation and $\widetilde X_h$ the interpolated fine solution, the pair error reads
\[
e_{H\to h}^{X,\;L^2} \;=\; \big\| \widetilde X_h - \mathcal{I}_{H\to h} X_H \big\|_{L^2(\Omega)},
\qquad
e_{H\to h}^{X,\;H^1\text{-semi}} \;=\; \big\| \nabla(\widetilde X_h - \mathcal{I}_{H\to h} X_H) \big\|_{L^2(\Omega)} .
\]
Vector/tensor seminorms use the canonical componentwise sum.
All integrals are evaluated with sixth-order quadrature on $\mathcal{T}_h$.
For a sequence of refinement pairs we obtain $\{(h_H,e_{H\to h}^{X,\bullet})\}$, where the error is evaluated on the fine mesh and associated with the coarser mesh-size estimate $h_H$.
Temporal errors are computed analogously on a fixed mesh by comparing consecutive timesteps and associating each pair error with the coarser step $\Delta t_H$.
The annotated slopes $p$ in \cref{fig:individual_convergence} are obtained from a log--log linear regression on the available refinement pairs.
