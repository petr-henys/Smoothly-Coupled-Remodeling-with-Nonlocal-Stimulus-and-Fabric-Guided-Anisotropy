\section*{Comparison with related models and novelty}
\label{sec:related-novelty}
Our formulation sits between classical density--only mechanostat models and fully coupled cell--population or damage--remodeling frameworks. In \emph{local} mechanostat models the remodeling drive is a pointwise function of a mechanical index, typically strain or strain--energy density, and only the density $\rho$ evolves; transport and fabric are absent and spatial regularity is supplied ad hoc (cf.\ early density models and follow--ups reviewed in \cite{Giorgio2019_MechanicallyDrivenBiological}). Nonlocal and \emph{diffusive} stimulus fields---as in \eqref{eq:stim-strong-nd}---have been advocated to account for osteocytic/vascular communication and to remove checkerboarding; this strand has gained traction in the last decade \cite{Giorgio2019_MechanicallyDrivenBiological, Addessi2024_BoneRemodelingApproach}. On the elasticity side, orthotropic models that couple BV/TV and second--order fabric tensors to the full fourth--order stiffness tensor (Cowin's relations and the Zysset--Curnier model) are now standard in homogenized micro--FE and hFE pipelines \cite{Cowin1985_RelationshipBetweenElasticity, Turner1990_FabricDependenceOrthotropic, Zysset1995_AlternativeModelAnisotropic, Maquer2015_BoneVolumeFraction, Maquer2017_NotOnlyStiffness}. Our constitutive choice preserves this spirit but evaluates a fabric-guided orthotropic law from the spectral decomposition of the log-fabric. Isotropic scaling is removed in log space and bounded before exponentiation, yielding controlled eigenvalue ratios and a smooth reduction to the isotropic baseline; the smooth density floor $\widetilde\rho\ge\rho_{\min}$ keeps the stiffness uniformly coercive, cf.\ \eqref{eq:smooth-max-nd}.

In the \emph{evolution} of anisotropy, several authors align fabric with stress or strain measures and regularize it by diffusion or smoothing (e.g.\ structure--tensor approaches and stress--alignment hypotheses, see \cite{Kuo2007_QuantifyingAnisotropyTrabecular, Rietbergen2015_NovelApproachEstimate, Dutra2016_QuantifyingTrabecularBone}). We follow this line by evolving the log-fabric $\mathbf{L}=\ln\mathbf{A}$ with a relaxation--diffusion flow toward a trace-free logarithmic target constructed from the cycle-averaged \emph{deviatoric} stress outer-product,
\(
\bar{\mathbf{Q}}=\mathrm{sym}(\boldsymbol{\sigma}_{\mathrm{dev}}\boldsymbol{\sigma}_{\mathrm{dev}}^{\!\top})
\)
with $\boldsymbol{\sigma}_{\mathrm{dev}}:=\boldsymbol{\sigma}-\tfrac13(\mathrm{tr}\,\boldsymbol{\sigma})\mathbf{I}$, using smooth regularization and eigenvalue-ratio bounds to avoid non-differentiable spectral projections and extreme anisotropy.
